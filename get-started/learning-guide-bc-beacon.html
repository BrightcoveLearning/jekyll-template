This learning guide provides a starting point for your understanding of Brightcove Beacon.

Introduction

Brightcove Beacon provides video providers the environment for their customers to view videos without having a cable provider subscription. Along with this core functionality, Brightcove Beacon also:

  • Allows customers to view videos on the devices that they already have, including mobile phones, tablets, connected TVs and Smart TVs.
  • Greatly reduces overall costs.The costs of developing apps for each platform are considerable, anywhere from $50K-$250K for a custom app targeted at just ONE platform. Brightcove Beacon allows creation of multiple apps from a single configured experience.
  • Provides a consistent experience for viewers across all platforms.
  • Allows monetization of video content via options like AVOD, SVOD and Pay Per View.

Brightcove Beacon provides this functionality and these features in what is referred to as an OTT environment. This learning guide will start by defining what OTT is.

What is OTT?

According to Wikipedia, over-the-top (OTT) media services are streaming media services offered directly to viewers over the Internet. OTT bypasses traditional cable, broadcast and satellite television platforms that traditionally act as a controller or distributor of such content. Some existing OTT services include Prime Video, Hulu and Netflix.

More simply, OTT is for companies who own or buy media of some type and wish to sell it for people to watch.

Not only can OTT content can be viewed from traditional desktop, laptop and tablet computers, but also needs to provide viewing from mobile phones, smart TVs (Samsung, LG, etc.) and set-top “boxes” (Apple TV, Fire TV, Roku, etc.).

VOD versus OTT

To be sure the difference between the two terms VOD and OTT, which can be confusing and often incorrectly considered to be the same thing, is understood, let’s clarify. VOD (video on demand) generally refers to video on demand services through cable networks. OTT on the other hand is transmitted to the viewer via the Internet without requiring users to subscribe to a traditional cable service.

Now that OTT is defined, it will be easier to understand what Brightcove Beacon provides in the space.

What is Brightcove Beacon?

Brightcove Beacon is a platform that enables you to create and configure an OTT experience, which is then used to generate multiple device apps. The device apps generated are grouped into the following five categories (some specific examples included):

  • Mobile (Phone / Tablets)
    • Android
    • iOS
  • Connected TVs
    • Android TV
    • Apple TV
    • Fire TV
    • Roku
  • Smart TVs
    • LG
    • Panasonic
    • Samsung
    • Sony
    • Toshiba
  • Web

Not only do you get multiple device apps from a single source, but Brightcove Beacon also allows you to control the content and layout for each device app from your single Brightcove Beacon Experience.

How is Brightcove Beacon implemented?

The phases listed in this table are detailed in the Brightcove Beacon Implementation Phases document.

Implementation Phase Description
Prepare for Brightcove Beacon Experience Creation* Gather design artifacts, create your app site map and plan strategies for advertising and monetization.
Establish Relationships with 3rd Party Vendors* Create accounts for: Stores to which you wish to submit device apps, advertising server, online payment vendor, etc.
Add Content and Configure Your Brightcove Beacon Experience Configuration of, and media assets added to, your Brightcove Beacon Experience.
Create Device Application(s) from Your Brightcove Beacon Experience Work with a Brightcove representative to finalize the configuration of your Brightcove Beacon Experience, from which the device apps are then generated.
Submit Your Device Applications to Corresponding Stores Submit the newly generated device app(s) to corresponding store(s) for approval, and then your apps are ready for customers’ use.
* The first two phases should be executed simultaneously.

For a more detailed explanation of the of the implementation phases see the Implementation Phases document.

How are the device apps updated?

Your specific device apps need only be submitted once to vendors for approval. Of course, you will want to change the media that is offered for viewing, but you may also wish to alter the app layout, or change your monetization for advertising strategy.
